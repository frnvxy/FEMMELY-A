Women Safety Matters

1 in 3 women globally experience physical or sexual violence in their lifetime.

--WHO, 2021

The power of collective safety

Peer-to-peer safety networks can reduce the "bystander effect," making people 40% more likely to intervene when they feel part of a connected community.

--Psychology Today, 2022

Environmental factors and perceived safety.

"Perceived safety" is heavily influenced by "eyes on the street." High-activity areas are statistically safer than isolated, poorly lit shortcuts.

--Urban Design Journal

The importance of rapid response.

In an emergency, the first 60 seconds are the most critical. Having pre-set emergency contacts reduces response time by an average of 5 minutes.

--National Safety Council

The data gap in women's safety.

Up to 80% of street harassment incidents go unreported to official authorities. Community reporting helps bridge this "dark figure" of crime statistics.

--UN Women

WHAT WE DO

Your Campus, Your Safety, Your Sisterhood.

Crowdsourced Safety Map

  • Allows verified women to drop color specified legends corresponding to it's level of safeness.
  • Displays subjective ratings for roads and hangouts based on lighting, crowd vibe, and security presence.

Lifestyle Directory

  • A consolidated page for women's needs, categorized into Sanitary Care, Beauty Products, and Services.
  • Helps users find specific items (e.g., "Where to buy napkins late at night").

The Sisterhood Forum

  • A restricted discussion board where verified users can seek advice or share experiences without fear of judgment.
